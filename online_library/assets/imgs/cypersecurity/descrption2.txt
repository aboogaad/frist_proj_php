Cybersecurity and Cyberwar : 

P.W. Singer and Allan Friedman
1988 - security - 481 
The book provides a comprehensive overview of the challenges and opportunities presented by the digital age, covering a wide range of topics relevant to both individuals and organizations.

The authors delve into the intricacies of cyber threats, from hacker attacks and data breaches to state-sponsored espionage and cyber warfare. They examine the evolving nature of cyber threats and the ways in which technology has transformed the landscape of conflict and security.

One of the book's strengths lies in its accessibility. Singer and Friedman break down complex concepts into understandable language, making the material accessible to readers regardless of their technical expertise. They provide real-world examples and case studies to illustrate key points, helping readers grasp the significance of cybersecurity issues in today's interconnected world.

The Art of Deception :

Kevin D. Mitnick and William L. Simon
1999 - security - 500
The book is structured around a series of engaging anecdotes and real-world examples that illustrate various techniques used by hackers to exploit human vulnerabilities. Mitnick masterfully demonstrates how seemingly innocuous interactions, such as a phone call or an email, can be leveraged to gain unauthorized access to sensitive information or systems.

Through these stories, readers gain insight into the tactics employed by social engineers, including pretexting, phishing, and tailgating, among others. Mitnick highlights the importance of skepticism and critical thinking in identifying and thwarting such attacks, emphasizing the need for organizations to invest in security awareness training for their employees.

2004 - security - 480

Dafydd Stuttard and Marcus Pinto
1999 - security - 306
The book covers a wide range of topics, including the anatomy of web attacks, common vulnerabilities such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS), and advanced exploitation techniques. Stuttard and Pinto provide detailed explanations of each vulnerability type, along with real-world examples and case studies to illustrate how they can be exploited in practice.

One of the key strengths of "The Web Application Hacker's Handbook" is its practical focus.
